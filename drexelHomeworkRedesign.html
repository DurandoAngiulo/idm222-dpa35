Drexel University Homework Site Redesign

A former professor reached out to me and a few other students work as a team under him to collaborate with the redesign and development our major's (UXID) homework website. This site was currently being used by all UXID(User Experience & Interaction Design) development courses as a way to reinforce new coding and development skills being taught in class.

ANALYSIS

Part I

Breaking down the current site

The first thing our team was directed to do was break down the current homework site. We had all already used this site for previous classes and our goal was to look for things to improve and fix with regards to functionality and aethetics.

DESIGN

Part II

Creating a Design System

Using Figma, our team then pooled all of our ideas, picking apart what we liked best from all of our concepts, and created a design system, covering everything from color & typography to iconography and page flow.

Creating a High-Fidelity Prototype

Next, the team put together our page structure making a hi-fidelity prototype of what the new site pages would look like.

DEVELOPMENT

Part III

Developingthe Site

After the new Homework Site designs were approved by our professor, with his oversight we then began working on the front end develeopment of the site using React. Prior to this assignment, none of the team had any React experience so development was often an uphill battle as we were learning and coding simultaniously. Luckily we still had our professor as an additional resource when we were confused (which was often!).

FINAL WEBSITE

Part IV

Take a look and explore the final look of the new Homework website, all developed using React!
